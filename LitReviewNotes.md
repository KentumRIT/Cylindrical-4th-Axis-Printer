### Input Parameters Investigated
- Speed
- Nozzle Temperature
- Layer thickness
### Output Parameters Investigated
- Peeling strength across 375 square mm of fabric
- They created a desirability factor optimizing for maximum peel force at the breaking point using the standard Derringer–Suich desirability transformation
### Methods Used
- The peeling force was measured with a 180 degree test standard called DIN EN ISO 8510-2 
- The fabrics were stretched by ~10% of their length and width to increase porosity and then taped to a bed for peel testing
- They standardized the peeling speed to 100 mm/s, a 0.05N preload and a 150 mm clamping length, all setup vertically for tests
### Useful Results
- Lower speed, higher nozzle temperature, and finer layer thickness proved to be optimal for adhesion strength
- Combination of 220 C nozzle temp, 30 mm/s speed, and 0.1 mm layer thickness required ~104N to be peeled over a surface area of 375 square milimeters acheiving a desirability factor of 0.893
